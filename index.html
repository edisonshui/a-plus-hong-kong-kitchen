Signature dishes · 招牌菜

What people come back for.

Eight dishes that get ordered, table after table. Start with a clay pot rice and a chow fun — you'll have covered Hong Kong's two most important food groups.

Our story · since 2009

A kitchen on 6th Avenue, run by the family that eats here.

The Wong family opened A+ Hong Kong Kitchen in 2009 in a corner shop on 6th Ave S — the same block our parents had been ordering from for two decades. The menu started with the twelve dishes auntie made on Sundays: clay pot rice, chow fun, fish steamed with ginger and scallion, a pot of milk tea.

Sixteen years later the menu has grown to eighty dishes and the dining room is louder, but we still cook the way she taught us. Stone pots over real flame. Noodles tossed in a screaming-hot wok. Egg tarts pulled from the oven every couple of hours. No shortcuts.

— Andy & Connie Wong

FAQ · 常見問題

Questions we get asked a lot.

  • What is clay pot rice (煲仔飯)?

    Clay pot rice is a Hong Kong specialty: rice cooked directly in a clay or stone pot over high heat, with toppings like Chinese sausage (lap cheong), marinated chicken, or salted fish added partway through. The rice at the bottom forms a crispy, golden crust — the best part — and the pot arrives sizzling at the table. Eat it right away, mixed with a splash of sweet soy.

  • Where are you located in Seattle?

    We're at 419 6th Ave S in Seattle's Chinatown–International District (the CID), one block from Uwajimaya, between S Jackson St and S King St. Light Rail Chinatown–International District station is a three-minute walk.

  • Do you take reservations?

    Walk-ins only. We don't take reservations — come in, put your name down, and we'll usually seat you within fifteen minutes. Larger parties (six or more) can call ahead at (206) 555-0140 and we'll do our best.

  • Do you have vegetarian or vegan options?

    Yes — stir-fried gai lan with garlic, dry-fried vegetable chow fun, mapo tofu (vegetable broth version on request), and stone pot tofu and mushroom rice. Ask your server and we'll point you to what's vegetarian-friendly.

  • Can I order online for pickup or delivery?

    Yes. Online pickup orders are usually ready in about twenty minutes. We deliver through DoorDash and Uber Eats, but pickup is faster and you skip the third-party fees.

  • Is there parking nearby?

    Street parking on 6th Ave S and S Jackson St (paid weekdays until 8 PM, free after and Sundays). The Uwajimaya garage is one block north and validates with a Uwajimaya purchase. We're also a three-minute walk from the Chinatown–International District Light Rail station.

  • What should a first-timer order?

    Baked spaghetti bolognese to start (it's a cha chaan teng classic — trust us), a stone pot rice with lap cheong, an order of dry-fried beef chow fun to share, a vegetable, and a cold milk tea each. Add an egg tart for after. That covers the whole cha chaan teng vocabulary in one sitting.
